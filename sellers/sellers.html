Sellers Guide

There are variety of reasons why people sell their homes, but every seller has one thing in common: the desire to get as much money as possible from their existing residence as quickly and as hassle-free as possible. (If you are selling your your principal residence, you won't have to pay tax on any profits from the sale. If, on the other hand, it is an investment property, gain is taxable)...
  • Make Your House More Sellable
    Your home might not be as appealing to every potential buyer(s) as it might be to you. Your professional sales representative will work with you to give you an objective analysis of your home - how it relates to other "competing" homes on the market and how your home reflects current design and style trends.
  • Stage your house
    Home staging is the act of preparing a private residence for sale in the real estate marketplace. The goal of staging is to make a home appealing to the highest number of potential buyers, thereby selling a property more swiftly and for more money.
  • Advertise Your Home on Various Internet Real Estate Portals
    Your home will be advertised on many Internet Real Estate Portals to ensure that the details of your home will be seen by a large number of potential buyers.
  • Arrange Showings
    As you start to bring your client interactions from website to real world, it’s time to prepare for the next big step: home showings. At this point, sales representative will prepare a home feature sheet(s). Home feature sheet(s) can be a one page or many pages synopsis of your home that highlights lot size, room dimensions, features and upgrades as well as utility costs and taxes.
  • The Viewing
    When a sales representative has a client who is interested in your home, they will first call to make an appointment with your sales representative.
